What is a DDOS in Technical Terms? - Part 1: The Botnet
So how does it reach this? How do thousands or millions of computers plan to gang up on one server?
Well there are 2 ways:
The first is that a large group of people will each download and use some piece of software to request a webpage hundreds of times per second. If enough people use the software they can successfully overload a server.
1
The second way is much more complex and involves a trick, an infection, and then the attack.
2
First the trick. Usually, a DDOS starts with a person who tricks many people into downloading a virus.
Second, the infection. The virus allows the person who issued it to control the infected computers remotely. Sometimes the computers are issued controls through IRC, the internet, or even Twitter status updates.
The computers become ‘bots’ and the person who created the virus becomes the ‘botmaster’.
The computers become ‘bots’ and the person who created the virus becomes the ‘botmaster’. As the botmaster acquires a large enough botnet, or network of bots, they can launch an attack on the website. The botmaster issues some command to request a webpage many, many times.

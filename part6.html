Section 6
Who Uses Them?

So who uses these attacks? Who is the botmaster?

?

Well, there isn’t just one botmaster. Anyone can be a botmaster, assuming that they have enough computers to make a botnet or the knowledge of how to acquire one.

Additionally, some botmasters sell their services to people who would like to run a DDOS but haven’t collected a botnet.

However, one common theme among people who use DDOS attacks is that they want to restrict access to a source of information.

Now given that governments typically have other means to restrict access at their disposal, they are usually not bot masters.*

*However, there are reasons to believe that some governments have used botnets to take down sites anonymously or to attack sites that are not under their control.

Infamous for its DDOSs is the hacktivist organization Anonymous, which has taken down many government and corporate websites using DDOS attacks.

So who gets DDOS’d?

?

Well, you don’t commonly see Google or Facebook going offline. The reason is because there are expensive methods to mitigate a DDOS; for large companies, DDOS’s can be managed.

Some methods include trying to discern real traffic from DDOS traffic by analyzing the requesters, the frequency of the requests, and what they are requesting -if they only order a glass of water many times, they are probably a bot.

Attacks are typically focused on small, activist sites which have a message that someone disagrees with.

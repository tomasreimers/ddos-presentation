Section 1
What are DDOS Attacks?

Imagine a restaurant.

It is a restaurant like any other, people come in and order all sorts of dishes: steak, burgers, anything. The waiter takes their order and brings them the food they requested. Each customer is served as the waiter becomes free.

1

Suddenly, hundreds or thousands of customers come in and order a glass of water.

2

The waiter becomes overwhelmed with the quantity of requests. As more customers enter the restaraunt the waiter is unable to attend to them because they are so overwhelmed.

3
That is a DDOS
making a resource unavailable by overloading it

It is a "Denial of Service". The server is never compromised, the databases viewed, nor the data deleted. Throughout and after the attack, the server remains intact.

The reason it is called a Distributed Denial Of Service is because there were many attackers. Each individual that ordered a glass of water was aiding in the attack.

Typically, the attackers don’t actually try to load a page, all they have to do is make a simple request for a glass of water, or maybe just the homepage. And if enough machines do this, they overload the server and prevent it from responding to legitimate requests.
